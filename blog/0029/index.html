Ray Dalio's Teachings
By Junho (August 2, 2023) back

Ray Dalio is one of the world's greatest macro investors. He has tremendous wisdom and knowledge. This post is essentially an index of his work which I've found useful.

Ray Dalio's work is incredibly dense. It's very little noise and almost pure signal - he packs a tremendous amount of information into a very few amount of words. Thus, I have to read his work very carefully to understand exactly what he's conveying in each phrase and sentence, and I recommend you do the same if you want to get the most value out of his reading his work.

Foundational Work

His seminal work that I'm most familiar with is his video called "The Changing World Order". He also has a book which I assume goes much more in-depth on the topic.

Ray also has published some other videos on Youtube such as "How the Economic Machine Works" which are quite educational.

He has a newsletter on LinkedIn (of all places?!) he's run for the last 3 years which I consider to be high-signal. Currently he has 42 installments of his newsletter where he talks about all things macro. Highly recommend going through every single installment in detail.

He also has an "articles" section on LinkedIn which has ~ 191 installations at the moment, which cover a great range of topics.

August 2, 2023 Market Update ("The Great Wealth Transfer")

An update post dated August 2, 2023, in which Ray explains why the latest QT isn't (yet) causing mass economic and market turmoil. Here is the link.

You can see from the incredibly comprehensive data visualizations in this post that Ray is one step above the typical macro investor. He's very methodical in his approach.

The most impactful quote I took away from this post was, "...the Fed accumulating bonds has been the norm, with two occasional and modest exceptions. I am watching this number closely because I believe the next significant increase in monetization will probably signal the last and probably biggest leg of the long-term debt cycle’s reduction in the values and burdens of debts".

As we know, the Fed had two massive money-printing stimulus:

  • First during the 2008 GFC
  • Second during the 2020 Covid crisis

What Ray is saying in his quote is that he believes the next round of money printing will coincide with the bursting of the sovereign debt bubble. In other words, the Fourth Turning.

People have been saying that the US fiscal situation is unsustainable but Ray is essentially making a specific call on the timing of things here.

Bitcoin

In January 28, 2021, Ray published a post on his thoughts about Bitcoin.

Ray provides a sober analysis of Bitcoin, along with reasonable doubts about potential existential risks.

My favorite quote from this article is "It seems to me that Bitcoin has succeeded in crossing the line from being a highly speculative idea that could well not be around in short order to probably being around and probably having some value in the future".

Ray understands that Bitcoin has "crossed the chasm", so to speak. This references the adoption chasm of a new product or thing.

Ray has publicly said that he has a small Bitcoin allocation, and I'm sure that allocation will be increasing, if it hasn't already.

Select articles

Why in the World Would You Own Bonds When...

  • "The problem is that, at current valuations, there is way too much money in these financial assets for it to be a realistic expectation that any significant percentage of that bond money can be turned into cash and exchanged for goods and services" - Ray's comment on 75 trillion dollars of US debt assets

The Mechanics of the War Economy

  • This is an incredible, incredible article that covers some recent conflicts (WWII, WWI, US Civil War) to observe how war changes economies. It's not only a history lesson but has a lot of economic wisdom
  • This also gives us a reference point of different economic states to which we can compare today's US economy and get some perspective on where in history we might be and might be going to

The Implications of Hitting the Hard 0% Interest Rate Floor

  • This article was written March 16, 2020, after Covid created an economic downturn but before governments around the world all implemented their monetary stimulus measures
  • As much as Ray has harped on the debt crisis it initially surprised me to see him recommending governments to create more debt and print more money to "address" this crisis
  • However I suppose Ray is just being pragmatic here and doing his best to provide actionable advice for policymakers who don't have an option of letting the economy fail because it would be political suicide to do so
  • It really shows how governments really are backed into a corner when it comes to this debt crisis

We Need Coordinated Monetary and Fiscal Stimulus to Counteract Coronavirus

  • Written March 10, 2020, a week before the above article. Again it's a bit strange to see Ray Dalio advocating for debt monetization. He does give a humanitarian justification for this in the article
  • An interesting tidbit from this article is Ray's distinction between monetary and fiscal policy. Monetary policy refers to actions that a (supposedly) independent banking system might take, and fiscal policy being the actions that a government would take

The World Has Gone Mad and the System Is Broken

  • Essentially Ray saying that the financial situation in the US is unsustainable. Written a few months before the Covid crisis.
  • TLDR - so much currency units but everyone is broke!

The Three Big Issues and the 1930s Analogue

  • Covers MP1 (interest rates), MP2 (printing money and buying financial assets), and MP3 (printing money and spending it)
  • Covers the rising of interest rates into the interest-rate blowoff-top in 1980-82, which coincided with the gold bull market
  • Mentions the subsequent lowering of interest-rates to 0 to modern day, which coincided with the bond bull market
  • Ray mentions that the extremely loose monetary policy over the last few decades was actually NEEDED to counteract strong deflationary forces (ex: globalization)
  • The entire dynamic I just described at a high-level is something I would love to become more familiar with

It’s Time to Look More Carefully at “Monetary Policy 3 (MP3)” and “Modern Monetary Theory (MMT)”

  • Breaking down various possible monetary and fiscal policy configurations of MP3, the stage we're in today
  • Observes various monetary and fiscal policies using during 1930s (an analogous period to today)

Excerpts from Principled Perspectives

How Can We Tell How the First Battle in the Long War for Control of the World Order Is Going?

  • I'm both impressed and surprised.
  • Written April 11, 2022, Ray understands exactly why this war is going on: "To me the Russia-Ukraine-US-other-countries dynamic is the most attention-grabbing part of the changing world order dynamic that is underway, but it is essentially just the first battle in what will be a long war for control of the world order". Otherwise it's a fantastic geopolitical summary of the conflict. Ray gets into various parts of the game tree considering various possible outcomes on a myriad of issues
  • Another great quote: "History has shown that when the causes people are behind are more important to them than the system (as they increasingly appear to be in the US), the system is in jeopardy".
  • Going through Ray's works, especially this one, is showing me that there is a great deal of nuance in geopolitics and macro investing
  • I realized Ray essentially has a large team at Bridgewater that is an extension of his brain and body. If he wants to get certain readings on the world, he just tasks one of his worker threads (employees) with doing it and they'll get back to him. This lets him spend his limited time and energy thinking at incredibly high levels of abstraction
  • Another great quote: "If they are not effective, we will risk seeing America lose its most unique and greatest power—its control over the world’s reserve currency and capital markets system—as others increasingly escape it. This a real big deal for the power of the United States and the power of the dollar. Without a strong sanctions power, the United States doesn’t have any unique powers relative to the opposition, though it would still have rough power parity with the other powerful countries. That leaves both sides, and a number of countries on each side, with just the power to inflict devastating destruction on the other side militarily. Given how many countries there are that have that power (i.e., how many have nuclear weapons) and given how the sides are lining up, this set of conditions looks frighteningly like those that existed before World War I except the destructive powers are much more “advanced.” That is a scary picture".
