Bitcoin Is Here To Stay
By Junho (July 22, 2023) back

Experienced people teach inexperienced people. But sometimes, experienced people are thinking at such a high level that they take for granted concepts or skills that they deem as basic that the beginner, in their infancy, does not yet understand.

The same is true for Bitcoin. Bitcoin maximalists understand very well that Bitcoin is the best money ever created and that Bitcoin adoption and hyperbitcoinization are self-fulfilling prophecies due to economic reality and human nature. Yet to those who are new to Bitcoin, these things are not obvious at all.

Those who are new to Bitcoin have all kinds of questions:

  • Why is Bitcoin here to stay?
  • Why is Bitcoin not just a fad or a scam?
  • Why won’t Bitcoin just suddenly go to 0?

Today, I’m here to provide answers to these questions. This won't be an economic study of Bitcoin that covers concepts such as the fiat scam or what sound money is, but rather a high-level response to various specific skepticisms that newcomers to Bitcoin have, and a look at the human incentives that drive Bitcoin adoption, as well as some historical data points.

Naysayers cannot naysay Bitcoin to death

People who don’t believe in Bitcoin cannot, through actions isolated to themselves:

  • make the price go down
  • stop curious people from learning about and adopting Bitcoin
  • convince Bitcoin maximalists to stop believing in Bitcoin

The only thing they can do is adopt Bitcoin, buy it, and make the price go up. They are literally just noise on the sidelines babbling away with their uniformed takes.

For naysayers to hurt Bitcoin, they have to step up from being naysayers and actually take steps to hurt and damage Bitcoin.

For the same reason that naysayers couldn't stop the adoption of electricity, motor vehicles, or the Internet, naysayers cannot prevent Bitcoin adoption.

Governments can’t ban Bitcoin to death

Okay, naysayers can't naysay Bitcoin to death. But governments are going to ban it, right? Surely they will, and surely that will kill bitcoin. Right?

First of all, Bitcoin is a global money. If the US government bans Bitcoin, it might temporarily hinder Bitcoin adoption in the US, but Bitcoin adoption will continue in all the other countries in the world, and may even speed up because the US government banning Bitcoin would actually validate Bitcoin as a credible force. Just a reminder, the US is only 4.2% of the global population. Bitcoin doesn't need the US.

For governments to kill Bitcoin by banning it, every government would have to coordinate to ban Bitcoin all over the world, completely destroy the code, locate all the miners and nodes and stop them from running, and completely kill the idea of Bitcoin. This would require an insane amount of authoritarion control, power, resources, and cooperation which governments around the world simply don't have.

Even if governments were to successfully accomplish this, they would have to maintain this extreme global authoritarian control forever, because eventually Bitcoin would just be recreated under some other name. Bitcoin isn't simply the monetary network that exists today - Bitcoin is an idea. Bitcoin in its current form today is just the first incarnation of digital sound money. If Bitcoin is killed (which is pretty much impossible) it will simply rise from the ashes at some later point in time, by humans who seek truth and that which is right.

Historically, China has actually tried to ban Bitcoin already. It failed to kill Bitcoin in any way whatsoever, for the obvious reasons I've just listed. Recently China is backpedaling on this and moving to embrace Bitcoin, for reasons of self-interest which I'll discuss in the next section.

Currently, parts of the US establishment, at first glance, seem to be trying to attack Bitcoin. However, these attacks are limited to crypto (which are pretty much all unregistered securities) and not Bitcoin. Even the SEC has acknowledged that Bitcoin is not a security. However I believe the US establishment, rather than trying to kill crypto, is simply attempting a takeover of it. Crypto happens to also be very difficult to kill for many of the same reasons as Bitcoin, although I believe crypto in its current casino state will eventually die during The Final Parabola.

Trying to kill Bitcoin by banning it is the same thing as the government trying to kill smartphones, the Internet, or electricity by banning it. It's ridiculous to think the government could kill those technologies, and it's ridiculous to think the government can kill Bitcoin. Bitcoin is a revolutionary technology. It can't be banned.

Governments will adopt Bitcoin, not ban it

Far from banning it, governments will actually increasingly move to adopt and accumulate Bitcoin for themselves, for very simple reasons of self-interest and economic gain and survival.

Since Bitcoin can't be killed, there is actually an incentive for governments to adopt Bitcoin before other governments:

Prisoners Dilemma for Bitcoin Adoption

The governments who adopt Bitcoin first will get a head start on accumulating Bitcoin, with the same for their citizens. First movers can also get head starts in creating Bitcoin infrastructure which will smoothen the transition to a Bitcoin standard.

From an even more simple perspective of hedging, even if governments consider the probability of hyperbitcoinization to be low, if such a probability exists, it would be prudent to have a small Bitcoin allocation to hedge against the situation of hyperbitcoinization happening without them. Of course, this will simply make other governments follow suit and kick off the whole process of governments adopting Bitcoin all over the world.

Looking at recent news:

  • America could use Bitcoin to leapfrog BRICS' imminent implementation of a gold standard, which would destroy US monetary dominance
  • El Salvador is the first government to move to a Bitcoin standard
  • Incremental Bitcoin mining adoption is happening in various countries around the world

Hedge funds will adopt Bitcoin

Blackrock, the world's largest (known) asset manager, has filed for a Bitcoin ETF, which will likely get approved since Blackrock is a major political player. In fact they likely got approval before they filed the ETF in the first place because Blackrock follows the orders of the head honchos up top.

What does this mean? It means there's a serious demand for Bitcoin allocation from the world's wealthiest investors.

And once Blackrock's Bitcoin ETF gets approved, many, many hedge funds and pools of institutional money around the world will follow suit.

It's no question that there are tons of people in powerful political positions who use Blackrock. Which brings me to my next point.

People with wealth and power will adopt Bitcoin

At the end of the day, great investors and those with power are people too, and will inevitably realize they need to hedge in Bitcoin or adopt it entirely. There are already many high-profile individuals who recognize Bitcoin’s merits and have at least dipped their toes into it.

This includes various high-profile establishment investors such as:

  • Bill Miller
  • Ray Dalio
  • Paul Tudor Jones
  • Stan Druckenmiller

RFK Jr., a popular Democratic US presidential candidate this upcoming 2024 election, also owns Bitcoin and has publically endorsed it as part of his campaign policy.

The list goes on and on; I won't even bother trying to list them all. Off the top of my head, Jack Dorsey, Telegram's founder, Elon Musk, and Michael Saylor have endorsed Bitcoin in their own ways.

Commonfolk will adopt Bitcoin

Commonfolk need sound money as much as anybody else. How else will you save your hard-earned money without the government stealing it from you via inflation?

This is especially true in countries with weaker currencies such as developing countries in South America, Africa, and Southeast Asia.

The US, some would say, is actually becoming a third-world country when it comes to having a weak currency with high inflation but that's a story for another time.

AI will adopt Bitcoin

I'm including this meme since AI has been a popular narrative recently but the thing is, it's not actually a meme. The only property that AI can possess without counterparty risk is digital self-custodied property protected by the laws of physics and math and that digital property happens to be Bitcoin.

What makes Bitcoin so relentless?

At its core, the "force of Bitcoin" is led by a core group of people who actually understand:

  • what money is
  • the issues with the current fiat monetary system
  • why Bitcoin is the best money ever created
  • that altcoins are worthless relative to Bitcoin and will never unseat it as money

These people have unwavering and rock-solid understanding and belief in Bitcoin. They understand that there is a great problem in the world and that Bitcoin solves that problem. They came to Bitcoin through various ways, such as seeking truth and opportunity, seeking solutions to problems, or by knowing other Bitcoiners.

This group is a group that will only continue to grow as more people get educated about Bitcoin. The truth is, Bitcoin is a profound creation that is the solution to many of society's problems today. There are so, so many roads that lead to Bitcoin.

Once you learn about Bitcoin and start to believe in it, you become a part of this group, and never stop believing in Bitcoin. It’s a one-way street.

What even is Bitcoin?

Is Bitcoin a coin? Is it a software? Is it a network? Is it a scam? A fad? Digital tulips?

The truth is, Bitcoin can be whatever you think it is. Everyone has their own take on it.

But as someone who understands Bitcoin, I think Bitcoin is, among other things:

  • a self-reinforcing, viral force
  • the most sound money ever created
  • the greatest economic opportunity most people today will have access to in their lifetimes
  • a beautiful idea
  • an amazing technological invention that will improve the world in so many ways

Bitcoin is a singularity.
