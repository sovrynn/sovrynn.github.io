2023 Banking Crisis Notes
By Henry J (March 27, 2023) back

This is just me taking notes on how banks work, and what is going on in today's banking crisis, at a very high level.

What is the business model of banks?

Banks lend out depositors' money to earn interest, and then pocket a portion of the interest as profit. That's their business model.

How does fractional reserve banking work?

In fractional reserve banking, banks only need to keep a fraction of their depositors' deposits as reserves. That means, at any given moment, if all depositors came to withdraw their money at the same time, the bank would be unable to pay out all the depositors with just the bank's reserves because the reserves are always less than the total deposits.

However, this doesn't mean the bank has lost all your money. Because eventually, the bank expects to get all the depositors' money back, plus interest, when the loans that the bank has made mature (assuming no credit risk). So the bank is basically "borrowing short" (from depositors) and "lending long". This is duration risk.

What is currently going on in the US banking system?

What is happening right now is slightly different from a typical bank run where a bank doesn't have enough reserves to pay out depositors. Basically, a lot of banks made long-term loans to the US government by investing in Treasuries, which are supposed to be the most secure and liquid debt in the world. However, after the Fed's rate hikes starting in 2022, the market value of those Treasuries dropped because higher interest rates result in lower bond prices. In the case of SVB, when depositors came to ask for their money, SVB couldn't dump their Treasuries for enough dollars to pay back all its depositors. It's basically still just duration risk ASSUMING that you expect the US to be in good health when the Treasuries mature.
