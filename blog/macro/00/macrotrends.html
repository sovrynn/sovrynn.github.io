The Biggest Force - The Digital World

From what I see, the biggest force affecting the 21st century is the digital world. Dominantly, this consists of the Internet, but also includes things like phone networks, IoT, blockchains, etc. The digital world allows for the rapid spread of information and knowledge. Importantly, while the digital world has grown massively over the last few decades, it is still in its very early stages. It is changing very fast.

There are also second-order effects here. The digital world allows for more efficient pricing of things in the very short-term, but it also creates butterfly effects that could cause humans to reach consensus or solve something analogous to the “prime number maze” for human society on longer timeframes. In other words, the digital world can be the platform of information on top of which new abstractions or ideas are developed which change human society in large ways.

Trend 1: Human liquidity increases

The idea for this trend is that humans, rather than having local knowledge and low mobility, have more global knowledge and higher mobility, and are more replaceable and interchangeable.

What do I mean by “liquid”? Investopedia defines a “liquid market” as a market “with many available buyers and sellers and comparatively low transaction costs”.

This is not a new trend. I think humans are already much more “liquid” today compared to even very recently in history. It’s only recently that global travel became cheap and easy. Even more recently, the Internet has allowed for the ability to share information with people all around the world. This naturally allows for better market pricing - you have trade and production globalizing. And of course this also applies to human capital, which is what this trend is referring to.

I think this trend continues. Travel could get marginally better, more people will continue to be plugged into the Internet, and the Internet will become more ubiquitous.

There’s a bit of a parallel here I want to bring up - Balaji coined an interesting concept called the “network state” which is a structure that could compete with the nation state. A shift of dominance to network states from nation states requires and implies increased human liquidity. Nation states impose restrictions on the mobility of their citizens, as well as the stay of foreigners.

Anyways back to increased human liquidity. What does it mean in terms of results? For one, jobs are more competitive - you’re competing with a global pool of talent, sometimes with far more to gain and far less to lose than you. Dating also becomes more liquid.

Trend 2 - Automation of Old Industries and Creation of New Industries

We have seen, are seeing, and will see automation of many industries. I’ll just rattle off a bunch:

  • Tax
  • Banking
  • Doctors giving simple prescriptions
  • Art (DALL E 2)
  • Writing
  • Repetitive manual labor (ex: factory work)

Anything that is digital, with structured inputs and outputs, can be automated by deterministic programming or machine learning. Entire industries (ex: banking) can be replaced by new systems (ex: crypto).

We also see the creation of new industries. Influencers? Streamers? Blockchain engineers? I need to see more of the world to be able to say more on this.

Increased abstraction due to improving technology capabilities will also shape industries. For example, artists might not be replaced but their work might look different. Instead of deterministically drawing things in Photoshop or some sketchpad, they might tweak machine learning models for art generation.

Automation generally takes power away from those who are automated, and gives power to those who can automate.
