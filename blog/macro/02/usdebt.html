US Debt Situation
By Henry J (April 18, 2023) back

Just organizing my mental model of the current US financial situation.

The US can't pay back its debts

Many countries don't want to lend to the US anymore, for various reasons.

  • America spends more than it earns, by a LOT, and borrows money to make up the difference.
  • America isn't profitable, and it has an enormous mountain of debt. It will almost certainly never be able to pay it back in real terms, because in order to do so, it has to start making more than it spends, and that's pretty much never going to happen at this point.
  • America has shown that it's willing to weaponize the dollar system when it froze Russia's forex reserves in response to Russia's invasion of Ukraine. You can't have a world reserve currency (USD) or world reserve asset (Treasuries) that can be frozen if you're on the wrong side of some country's agenda. Money must be apolitical.

The US defaulting will cause a global recession

The default of the US is all but a certainty. But this will hurt every country, because all countries, even China, are hooked into the USD money printer at the Fed. Economies around the world are very interconnected.

If the US defaults, who are the winners and who are the losers?

The debt of the world needs to be paid back eventually. When the US defaults, that means it was already paid. It was paid by all the people who had their wealth and incomes inflated away through dollar printing at the Fed. Those holding the bag will have nothing. When you borrow money without repaying, that is simply a transfer of wealth. Or theft.

The US government took the wealth of all the world, including its own citizens, by printing dollars! It says it's going to pay back the dollars someday but it just keeps printing more! When is the US going to pay back the dollars?

Do businesses and real estate maintain purchasing power in a US default?

The only things that maintain value are those things that maintain their purchasing power.

Over the recent years, things like real estate and businesses have typically maintained purchasing power.

However, much real estate is significantly overvalued, and has only preserved purchasing power because people use it as money (a store of wealth).

The same is true for businesses. Many stocks have only maintained purchasing power because they've been monetized, feed off off of cheap credit, or are massively overvalued. In a US default, these will all go to zero.

At the end of the day, a big chunk of real estate's value, and much of the US stock market (which is based on the US debt-based economy) are simply debt.

Sound money fixes this, of course. No need to take risk on a business which you don't understand, or buy a home you must maintain, to preserve purchasing power. Unbacked fiat money that constantly gets debased makes everyone a speculator, out of necessity, to maintain purchasing power.
