Use It Or Lose It
By Junho (August 18, 2023) back

Paradoxically:

  • eating too much food gives you less energy, not more
  • being active gives you more energy, not less

An object in motion tends to stay in motion. We are like sharks.

The parallel of debt and high blood sugar

It's the same as debt. An economy can get through a little debt once and be fine. Twice, sure. But the longer and longer large amounts of debt stay in the system, the more the economy becomes dependent on debt, and is actually UNABLE to sustain itself without it. This is because eventually, all the production is fueled by unsustainable debt instead of sustainable spending, and people start doing "finance" instead of actually being productive. The debt is like a parasitical cancer that grew so much that it made the entire economy into debt. If debt were to suddenly be removed from such an economy, it might have no demand as well as no productivity. Such an economy would struggle initially until it could rebuild its productive capacity and sustainable demand. This is the trough of the long-term debt cycle that Ray Dalio describes.

In the same way, if you fuel your body with copious amounts of blood sugar everyday, your body loses its ability to generate energy through natural processes and from stored fat. That process becomes more and more alien and uncomfortable. The moment you take away the excessive carb input, you'll realize that the actual baseline energy that your body can produce and is producing is extremely and unnaturally low. You'll be lethargic and unable to get anything done in the short-term without pumping yourself up with carbs again.

If you pair this with a sedentary lifestyle, it's a double whammy because it makes your energy production (and consumption) lower, making you more dependent on food for energy or motivation and also making you gain weight much more quickly.

Just like any other biological process, building up your energy production is a compounding process and doesn't happen overnight. Additionally, it's not something that you achieve once and then it just maintains itself forever. It needs a CONSTANT force backing it up, so what really needs to happen is you need to make these changes part of your lifestyle and DAILY routine.

Takeaways

  • Don't overeat and overcharge your body with blood sugar. Avoid excessive restaurant food
  • Regularly (everyday?) allow your body to run out of blood sugar, get hungry, and tap into fat reserves so your body is efficient, familiar, and comfortable with that process
  • Don't have a sedentary lifestyle. Make your body produce energy by being active around the clock, every day
