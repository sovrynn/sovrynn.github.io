3/X Naval on Wealth (People and Relationships)
By Henry J (November 20, 2022) back

This post contains wisdom on how to think about people and relationships, and what kind of people you want to work with and keep in your life.

10. Play long-term games with long-term people. Compound interest applies not just in investing but also for people and relationships

Compound interest applies not just in investing, but also for people and relationships. In business, the longer you know someone, the more you can build trust and reduce the friction of doing deals. In dating, if you know and trust someone for long enough, you can get married, have kids, and raise children. These long-term relationships are invaluable compared to more casual relationships.

The thing is, in order to reap the benefits of compound interest, you have to play long-term games. For a career, this means you want to be in an industry where you know people are going to stick around, incentivizing people to build trust with each other, work together, and do right by each other.

Naval says a great example of this is Silicon Valley. In Silicon Valley, people are doing business with each other, know each other, and trust each other, so they do right by each other, because they know this person will be around for the next game (There are exceptions, like when one person thinks they can make enough money in one iteration of the game so they’re fine with betraying others).

If you keep switching industries, or burn all your bridges every time you move around, you lose the benefits of compound interest. Every time you wander out of where you built your network, you won’t know who to trust, and people won’t trust you. You stop the relationships before they can begin to compound.

In long-term games, people are making each other rich. But in short-term games, people are making themselves rich. In long-term games, people are creating a bigger pie for everyone over time. In short-term games, people are figuring out how to divide the pie for a short-term reward.

11. Pick people to work with who have high intelligence, energy, and integrity

Naval says this is his checklist that he can’t compromise on when picking people to work with:

  • They need to be smart because if they’re not, they’re going to be heading in the wrong direction
  • They need to be high-energy. Why? Well, the world is full of smart but lazy people. And they still need to be smart, because otherwise they’ll be going high-energy but in the wrong direction
  • Finally, high integrity is the most important, because with high intelligence and energy but low integrity, what you have is a smart and hardworking crook who’s eventually gonna cheat you.

There are caveats for the first two conditions. Smart is not necessarily measured on a single axis - people can be smart at different things. And for having high energy, people can have different amounts of energy for different things. Someone might be really unmotivated to go sit at the office and work a job but might be really motivated to go paint, for example. Then, this person should go be a painter and put up art on the Internet or something.

The way you figure out whether someone has high integrity is through signals. Signals are what they do, not what they say. It’s all the nonverbal stuff that people do when they think no one is looking.

12. How do you pick the right person to pick to do a specific task? They need intrinsic motivation

Naval says he won’t start a company or hire a person or work with somebody if he doesn’t think they’re into what he wants them to do. When he was younger, he tried to talk people into things. It might get them inspired initially. But he found it doesn’t work to keep them motivated for the long-term. You can’t create long-term motivation for someone, nor can you be the crutch for them. It has to be intrinsic. So you have to make sure people are actually high energy and actually want to do what you want them to do.

13. How do you figure out how people actually are? What their true self is?

You have to learn how to read signals. Signals are what people do, despite what they say. It’s important to pay attention to subtle signals to figure out how people actually are.

For example, if someone treats a waiter badly, then that’s just how they are and how they think, and it’s only a matter of time until they treat you badly. If someone is very vindictive to an enemy, then it’s only a matter of time until they think of you as an enemy and treat you badly. People are oddly consistent.

One specific thing you can use signals to figure out is whether someone has high-integrity. You want to find people who are long-term people; who seem irrationally ethical. Because then, that integrity is likely to be a deep part of who that person is, and then you will be able to trust them and work with them. These kinds of people tend to have very high self-esteem. Because self-esteem is the reputation you have with yourself. And good people, moral people, ethical people, reliable people, will know what kind of people they are.

One signal that signals someone has low integrity is if they are saying they are moral and ethical and high-integrity. This is like status signaling - if you overtly bid for status, that is actually an action that low status people do.

13. Partner with Rational Optimists, not Cynics or Pessimists

To create things, you have to be a rational optimist. You have to see the world for what it really is, yet at the same time be optimistic about your ability to achieve your goals and get things done.

You don’t want to be with, or partner with, people who are just critics and downers on everything. These people will not only never do anything great in their lives, they will also prevent people around them from being great. They will just shoot holes in things. It’s okay to shoot holes in things, but only if you provide solutions. But these people want to get in your way and tell you why you can’t do something while at the same time not providing any solutions.

Pessimists and cynics are really just people who have given up and don’t think they can do anything. So to them, the world just looks like a world where nobody can do anything. And to them, no one else should go do anything, because if they fail, the pessimist is right, but if they succeed, then the pessimist just looks bad.

Successful people have action bias. They just do things. The easiest way to figure out if something is viable or not is by doing it. At least try the first, second, and third steps and then decide. Have an action bias towards getting what you want. And you have to be optimistic about it. Not irrationally - then you will be chasing something in a foolhardy way.

Optimism is not just about believing you can achieve some goal but also believing you can achieve a big goal. It takes a lot of work to even build small things. The owner of the corner grocery store probably isn’t working any less hard than Elon Musk. They just didn’t think as big, for whatever reason (education), so the outcome isn’t as big. If you can direct the same amount of work at any task you desire, why not direct that work at large tasks that can have lots of impact? You only have one life on this planet after all.

There’s an interesting thought experiment that explains why we’re innately pessimists but also why it makes sense to be optimistic today. Long ago in the jungle, if there was a possibility of a tiger coming, the pessimist would GTFO of the situation because they could die, but the optimist would say it’s not headed their way, stay, and get eaten. So we’re descended from pessimists. Pessimists manage risk of the most negative outcome, and that way, they can protect themselves from ruin and maximize their chances of survival. But today, society and the world is far far safer. It’s very unlikely that you will end up in total ruin. It’s much more likely that the upside is unlimited, and the downside is limited. So you should overcome your innate pessimism and take slightly irrational but optimistic bets because the upside is unlimited. It made sense to be pessimistic in the past, but it makes sense to be optimistic today.

The one thing you do have to avoid is complete ruin. Stay out of jail, and stay out of total catastrophic loss. Watch your healthy/body/safety, and watch out for things that could cause you to lose all of your capital.
