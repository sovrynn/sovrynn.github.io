A (Hopefully) Level-Headed Bitcoin Analysis
By Junho (July 11, 2024) back

I am a baka. A being of the flesh. A human. A man. And no more.

Through the last two years of being a maximalist, I wrote many words, which at the time, seemed like reasonable proofs that Bitcoin must absolutely succeed.

Yet today, as I realize the limitations of my flesh, it dawns on me that all the words I've written may just have been fancy rationalizations of my own fantasies.

In this post I hope to discard my emotional fantasies, lay down a stable equilibrium position I can maintain for many years, and tame my wild passions so that I can focus on productive tasks.

Bitcoin is but a tool, it's necessary but insufficient

One of my gripes with Bitcoin maximalist culture is that it treats money as a God. There is this meme that money is upstream of all societal problems, and that sound money will flip society on its head. I disagree.

I think that money is just one small part of the bigger picture. Politics, morality, religion, war, race, technology, transhumanism, the environment, humanity's future, culture, and so on. No, I don't think that sound money will solve everything. I also don't imply that Bitcoiners all think this way; I think that's a strawman. But I simply note that in the public Bitcoin discourse, I don't see such topics placed at the forefront. There is simply a focus on sound money's benefits, which certainly makes sense for the movement as a whole, since it can resonate with the greatest amount of people by being unbiased and focused in its messaging. But hence, my point - it's just one piece of the bigger puzzle.

I also dislike the materialism, greed, and individualism that are normalized in Bitcoin culture. The idea that you will become wealthy and be able to sit on your stack while all the other poors will have to work to earn their Bitcoin from you. The get rich relatively-quickly meme. People talking about the things they'll buy when Bitcoin hits so-and-so price. Being able to quit their jobs. "Everyone buys Bitcoin at the price they deserve".

Sure, perhaps it's just healthy toxicity. Righteous aggression. Manipulative marketing. For me, whatever it all is, it's insufficient, and not maximally relevant.

There are many uncertainties in Bitcoin

Bitcoin can be undermined through code changes. At the moment, physics is still much more robust than software. Additionally, advancements in computing may undermine Bitcoin's scarcity or fundamental features.

At best, Bitcoin is...

Reasonably decentralized, reasonably secure, and reasonably scarce.

Final Parabola?

About an year ago I wrote a "Bitcoin endgame thesis" where I expected Bitcoin to reach fiat prices of infinity likely within a decade. If you asked me back when I wrote it, I would have said I expect it to happen within the given timeframe (5-15 years) with high (80%+) probability.

Do I still think so? No.

Do I think it's a non-trivial possibility? Yes.

Do I think there's any way to put a high-probability estimate on exactly how long it'll take? No.

Do I think it could plausibly take a century or even two centuries? Absolutely.

The process through which the Final Parabola will happen is nothing more than the summation of individual choices of all humans on the planet. The more people believe in Bitcoin, the more nominal amount of people can be onboarded, by imagining a simple exponential growth process.

I think the real amount of convicted, money-where-their-mouth-is Bitcoiners (maximalists) is far less than the tens and hundreds of millions that people model using silly metrics like address counts, owning any amount of Bitcoin, and shitcoin casino enlistment metrics. I think the total number of convinced Bitcoiners could reasonably be 100k, as a generous estimate, give or take an order of magnitude. By "convinced" Bitcoiners I mean true maximalists, not speculators who are dipping their toe in.

Point being? We are far, far away from 1% "adoption" (~80 million people). It's still early - far away from an inflection point on the adoption curve.

Additionally, while the fiat price chart looks like an inverted S-curve, I haven't yet been able to imagine what the relative purchasing power graph will look like over time. There are too many variables for me to accurately predict anything. It's a distinct possibility that most of the purchasing power gains will happen in a diminishing returns fashion, and the second half of the fiat price chart will simply be the collapse of fiat purchasing power. A dramatic rise in the Bitcoin fiat price accompanied by a dramatic fiat hyperinflation.

It's all a faraway possibility. Of course, the longer Bitcoin remains standing, the more maximalists will be christened, and the more probable the Final Parabola becomes.

An aside - exponential growth model

Based on my generous assumption of about 100k current maximalists and using a simple exponential maximalist growth model, we can calculate how long it'll take for the whole world to onboard.

It took 15 years to go from Satoshi to 100k, which gives an yearly exponential growth rate of 2.1544.

Based on this, it will take another 14.7 years for the world population of ~8 billion to become maximalists.

If the current number of true maximalists is 10k (another reasonable guess) it'll take 22.1 years.

Food for thought. Humans are memetic herd creatures as a whole, so an exponential model is very reasonable.

And yet, Bitcoin still fills me with hope

So, there you have it. My "level-headed analysis" of Bitcoin. Many uncertainties. No caricature "sound-bite" hopium fantasy Bitcoin maximalism. This uncertainty is as certain as it gets for me at this moment.

Despite all this, I still trust Bitcoin more than any fiat asset, any business, any piece of land, any single jurisdiction. And I know the same can be said for many maximalists out there. And that alone, gives me hope, and conviction.

What do they say? Actions speak louder than words... and the exception proves the rule. Even in the greatest moments of doubt, Bitcoin stands like a shining beacon of hope, alone. It has no equal.
